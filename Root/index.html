Research
Li-koul blassa malik’ha
Intersecting gazes in travel photography
Projects
Elodie Sacher is a researcher specializing in contemporary visual culture and photographic archives from Morocco. She is particularly interested in the representation and staging of rulership through portraiture, in both analogue and digital forms. Her work on royal portraiture and tourist snapshots traces the history of photography and its entanglement with gestures of authority and power in the Western Maghreb, drawing on a range of sources including vernacular photography, institutional archives and popular media culture.

She studied Art history and Cultural studies in Hildesheim, Casablanca, Berlin and Jena and currently works at the Department of Digital Humanities at the FSU Jena and as a project manager and researcher for A.MAL Projects and 560Zoom.

Write to elosacher@gmail.com for enquiries, research hints, archive material and collaborations.
